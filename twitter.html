what it means:
anything you post is tied to you. don’t tweet about crimes you have or will commit unless you are comfortable sharing that with the world and admitting to it. don’t tweet misleading information, like “masks don’t work!!11!” since you are responsible for it.

what it means:
twitter will not take responsibility for anything you see. tweets you see are not always fact-checked and you should be careful with posts like “click here to get $1000!!” or "trump won the election!!"

what it means:
twitter has the right to the content or information you put on twitter if needed by law (ex: law enforcement needs to know if you were present at the murder scene), enforcing their terms, looking for and stopping fraud or security breaches (ex: if your account is being used for a virus or scam), investigating user reports about you (ex: if someone reports your account, twitter looks into why), and protecting rights for twitter, its users and the public.
what it says:
you are responsible for safeguarding your account, so use a strong password and limit its use to this account. we cannot and will not be liable for any loss or damage arising from your failure to comply with the above.
what it means:
have a strong password! twitter will not be taking responsibility for damage that may come from a hack. this can happen in situations such as having the same password for your bank account and twitter, so a hacker easily gets your information and takes your money.
what it says:

what it means:
to not be in this contract with twitter, you must deactivate your account and stop using twitter. twitter may also suspend your account for no reason or for the reasons mentioned: violations of the terms, you create possible legal problems for twitter, unlawful conduct, inactivity, or having your account with twitter is bad business wise for them.
privacy policy
key takeaways:
– twitter and your tweets are public and anyone can look them up. you can use twitter privately through a private account and direct messages. pseudonyms welcome! feel free to call yourself whatever you want!
– twitter gets information like the device you are on and your IP address while you scroll through your timeline. you can share your email address, phone number, contacts, and public profile to keep your account safe and to let twitter show you recommended tweets, people to follow, and tailored ads based on your information.
– you can change settings to limit data collected from your account such as location, a personalized profile twitter wants to make about you, and data shared to their business partners. this can be found in settings, privacy and safety, then personalization data.
– twitter uses your tweets, tweets you read, likes, retweets and “other information” to determine information about you. this includes guesses for your age, languages you speak, or topics you’re interested in to show you even more relevant content. you can modify these guesses in account settings.
– search engines and third parties may have copies of your public information even after something is deleted or your account has been deactivated.
– twitter does not support the do not track cookies browser option
– twitter prohibits personalized ads based on categories that they consider sensitive or are prohibited by law such as race, religion, politics, sex life, or health.
